# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

UnVibe is a learning community. Everyone here — whether asking their first question or maintaining core infrastructure — deserves to learn without harassment, condescension, or gatekeeping.

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive environment regardless of experience level, age, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

## Expected Behavior

- **Be constructive.** In peer reviews, War Rooms, and discussions — lead with what works, then suggest what could improve. Critique code, not people.
- **Be patient with learners.** The entire point of UnVibe is that people are building understanding. Nobody here is expected to already know the answer.
- **Give credit.** If you build on someone else's idea, module, or feedback — acknowledge it.
- **Respect different skill levels.** A rebuild that's simpler than yours isn't worse. A question you find basic isn't stupid.
- **Stay on topic.** Keep discussions relevant to the project, the code, or the learning process.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks of any kind
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome advances
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Dismissing questions or contributions based on someone's experience level
- Sustained disruption of discussions, War Rooms, or community spaces
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all UnVibe spaces: the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, War Rooms, community channels, and any other spaces managed by the project. It also applies when an individual is representing UnVibe in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior can be reported by contacting the project maintainers at **[your-email@example.com]**.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated. The project team is committed to maintaining confidentiality regarding the reporter. Responses may include:

1. **Correction** — A private conversation clarifying the violation and expected behavior.
2. **Warning** — A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior.
3. **Temporary Ban** — Temporary removal from community interaction.
4. **Permanent Ban** — Permanent removal from all project spaces.
